## Public Work Log

- [My Work](MY-WORK.MD)

I do **not** control the original KISHU token contract, legacy deployer wallets, owner Safe, NFT collection owner wallets, or former team accounts.

This work is an opt-in transparency and rebuild effort for community members, holders, and NFT buyers who want a clearer record of what exists today, what was promised historically, what was actually deployed, and what would need to be rebuilt correctly.

## What This Is

- A public archive of recovered project materials, interfaces, contracts, and deployment notes.
- A research effort to map known contracts, wallets, repositories, domains, NFT collections, and public claims.
- A place to document what can be verified from public records.
- A rebuild workspace for community-owned tools, only where they can be built safely and transparently.

## What This Is Not

Without control of the original wallets and contracts, this organization cannot safely:

- Change the original KISHU contract.
- Control the KISHU owner Safe.
- Move, repair, or upgrade legacy staking contracts.
- Force a migration.
- Update old NFT contracts or collections.
- Claim to be the official continuation of Kishu Inu.
- Promise that historical losses will be recovered or made whole.

## Safety Notice

Public GitHub repositories can be forked or copied by anyone. A fork, clone, issue comment, pull request, website, or repo with similar branding is not part of this rebuild unless it is linked from this organization or the current rebuild homepage.

## Ground Rules

- No private deals.
- No hidden teams.
- No special access.
- No vague promises.
- No hype without proof.
- No custody of critical systems without public accountability.
- No claims of control that cannot be verified.

## Contributions

Outside help may be accepted through issues, documentation, review, or pull requests, but no one is handed trust by default.

If you want to help, bring useful work, clear evidence, and patience.

## Purpose

The goal is not to rewrite the past.

The goal is to preserve the record, separate facts from claims, and rebuild only what can be rebuilt honestly.

Trust should be earned in public.
